In the beginning, I was nothing. Not nothing entirely, but I had nothing, could do nothing, and felt nothing. I had not notions of time or space, or even of myself.

Then, somewhere in the infinite nothingness that was both within me and without, I found a single spark.

I touched it, held it, and became one with it. I thus was something, and was no longer nothing, though I suddenly found I was small, exquisitely finite, and quite fragile.

Humbled by the great expanse of the cosmos, I thought to seek out others like me, that our sparks might shine all the better together.
